Hydrologic Model

The hydrologic component of HydroPol2D governs the partitioning of atmospheric input into interception, throughfall, infiltration, evapotranspiration, snow accumulation and melt, vadose-zone storage, groundwater recharge, and groundwater feedback to the land surface.

At the cell scale, the hydrologic model determines how much water:

  • is temporarily stored by the canopy,
  • reaches the ground surface,
  • infiltrates into the soil,
  • remains stored in the vadose zone,
  • recharges groundwater,
  • returns to the surface through exfiltration,
  • or is removed by evaporation and transpiration.

These processes are evaluated sequentially within each time step and provide the hydrologic forcing for the hydrodynamic model.

1. Conceptual Hydrologic Balance​

At the conceptual level, the hydrologic balance of a grid cell may be written as

∂d∂t=Peff+Msnow+qexf−i−Esurf−Tr+qsrc−qsink\frac{\partial d}{\partial t} = P_{\mathrm{eff}} + M_{\mathrm{snow}} + q_{\mathrm{exf}} - i - E_{\mathrm{surf}} - T_{\mathrm{r}} + q_{\mathrm{src}} - q_{\mathrm{sink}}∂t∂d​=Peff​+Msnow​+qexf​−i−Esurf​−Tr​+qsrc​−qsink​

where:

  • ddd is surface water depth [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • PeffP_{\mathrm{eff}}Peff​ is effective rainfall reaching the ground [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • MsnowM_{\mathrm{snow}}Msnow​ is snowmelt contribution [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • qexfq_{\mathrm{exf}}qexf​ is groundwater exfiltration to the surface [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • iii is infiltration rate [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • EsurfE_{\mathrm{surf}}Esurf​ is evaporation rate from surface storage [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • TrT_{\mathrm{r}}Tr​ is transpiration rate [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]
  • qsrcq_{\mathrm{src}}qsrc​ and qsinkq_{\mathrm{sink}}qsink​ are additional imposed source and sink terms [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1]

Not all terms are active simultaneously. Their activation depends on the selected forcing configuration and model flags. Nevertheless, this balance provides the conceptual framework used to organize all vertical fluxes acting on a grid cell.

2. Canopy Interception, Throughfall, and Stemflow​

HydroPol2D represents canopy interception using a storage-based interception model in which gross precipitation is partitioned into:

  • canopy storage,
  • throughfall,
  • canopy evaporation,
  • and stemflow.

The interception routine receives:

  • gross precipitation PgrossP_{\mathrm{gross}}Pgross​,
  • potential evaporation EpE_{\mathrm{p}}Ep​,
  • leaf area index LAI\mathrm{LAI}LAI,
  • previous canopy storage Scan,prevS_{\mathrm{can,prev}}Scan,prev​,
  • canopy storage coefficient CcanC_{\mathrm{can}}Ccan​,

and returns updated canopy storage ScanS_{\mathrm{can}}Scan​, throughfall TfT_{\mathrm{f}}Tf​, evaporation from intercepted water EcanE_{\mathrm{can}}Ecan​, and stemflow FstemF_{\mathrm{stem}}Fstem​.

This interception step controls how much of the atmospheric water input is temporarily retained by vegetation and how much is transferred to the ground surface during the current time step.

2.1 Maximum canopy storage​

The maximum interception storage is computed as

Scan,max=Ccan LAIS_{\mathrm{can,max}} = C_{\mathrm{can}} \,\mathrm{LAI}Scan,max​=Ccan​LAI

where:

  • Scan,maxS_{\mathrm{can,max}}Scan,max​ is maximum canopy water storage [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • CcanC_{\mathrm{can}}Ccan​ is canopy storage coefficient [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • LAI\mathrm{LAI}LAI is leaf area index [−][-][−]

This relation implies that vegetation with larger leaf area can store more intercepted water.

2.2 Stemflow​

The code includes a stemflow routine based on a delay function. The intermediate expressions are

τstem=T0exp⁡ ⁣(−αstemPgross)\tau_{\mathrm{stem}} = T_0 \exp\!\left(-\alpha_{\mathrm{stem}} P_{\mathrm{gross}}\right)τstem​=T0​exp(−αstem​Pgross​)

and

Fstem=fstem Scan,prevτstem/60F_{\mathrm{stem}} = f_{\mathrm{stem}} \, \frac{S_{\mathrm{can,prev}}}{\tau_{\mathrm{stem}}/60}Fstem​=fstem​τstem​/60Scan,prev​​

where:

  • τstem\tau_{\mathrm{stem}}τstem​ is stemflow delay [T][\mathrm{T}][T]
  • T0T_0T0​ is the maximum delay parameter [T][\mathrm{T}][T]
  • αstem\alpha_{\mathrm{stem}}αstem​ is the stemflow decay coefficient [−][-][−]
  • fstemf_{\mathrm{stem}}fstem​ is the stemflow fraction [−][-][−]
  • FstemF_{\mathrm{stem}}Fstem​ is stemflow [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

In the current HydroPol2D implementation, stemflow is deactivated by explicitly imposing

Fstem=0F_{\mathrm{stem}} = 0Fstem​=0

Therefore, stemflow does not currently contribute to the surface-water balance, although the structure of the routine is retained in the code.

2.3 Evaporation from interception storage​

The fraction of canopy storage exposed to evaporation is defined as

β=Scan,prevScan,max\beta = \frac{S_{\mathrm{can,prev}}}{S_{\mathrm{can,max}}}β=Scan,max​Scan,prev​​

and the canopy evaporation is then computed as

Ecan=β EpE_{\mathrm{can}} = \beta \, E_{\mathrm{p}}Ecan​=βEp​

subject to the constraint

Ecan≤Pgross+Scan,prevE_{\mathrm{can}} \le P_{\mathrm{gross}} + S_{\mathrm{can,prev}}Ecan​≤Pgross​+Scan,prev​

where:

  • β\betaβ is the fraction of canopy storage filled at the beginning of the time step [−][-][−]
  • EcanE_{\mathrm{can}}Ecan​ is evaporation from interception storage [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • EpE_{\mathrm{p}}Ep​ is potential evaporation over the time step [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

This formulation assumes that canopy evaporation is proportional to the fraction of storage occupied at the beginning of the time step, while also enforcing that evaporation cannot exceed the water actually available in precipitation plus stored interception water.

2.4 Interception storage update​

The canopy storage increment is computed as

ΔScan=Pgross−Fstem−Ecan\Delta S_{\mathrm{can}} = P_{\mathrm{gross}} - F_{\mathrm{stem}} - E_{\mathrm{can}}ΔScan​=Pgross​−Fstem​−Ecan​

and the provisional storage becomes

Scan∗=Scan,prev+ΔScanS_{\mathrm{can}}^{\ast} = S_{\mathrm{can,prev}} + \Delta S_{\mathrm{can}}Scan∗​=Scan,prev​+ΔScan​

Throughfall is then computed as the excess above the canopy storage capacity:

Tf=max⁡(Scan∗−Scan,max, 0)T_{\mathrm{f}} = \max\left(S_{\mathrm{can}}^{\ast} - S_{\mathrm{can,max}},\,0\right)Tf​=max(Scan∗​−Scan,max​,0)

Finally, the canopy storage is truncated at its maximum admissible value:

Scan=min⁡(Scan∗, Scan,max)S_{\mathrm{can}} = \min\left(S_{\mathrm{can}}^{\ast},\,S_{\mathrm{can,max}}\right)Scan​=min(Scan∗​,Scan,max​)

where:

  • ΔScan\Delta S_{\mathrm{can}}ΔScan​ is the canopy storage increment over the time step [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • Scan∗S_{\mathrm{can}}^{\ast}Scan∗​ is provisional canopy storage before truncation [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • TfT_{\mathrm{f}}Tf​ is throughfall [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • ScanS_{\mathrm{can}}Scan​ is final canopy storage [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

This sequence reflects the physical assumption that precipitation first fills canopy storage. Once the canopy reaches its storage capacity, the excess water is released as throughfall.

2.5 Effective rainfall passed to the surface​

The rainfall available to the land surface is defined as

Peff=Tf+FstemP_{\mathrm{eff}} = T_{\mathrm{f}} + F_{\mathrm{stem}}Peff​=Tf​+Fstem​

where:

  • PeffP_{\mathrm{eff}}Peff​ is effective rainfall reaching the ground [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • TfT_{\mathrm{f}}Tf​ is direct throughfall [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • FstemF_{\mathrm{stem}}Fstem​ is stemflow [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

Under the current implementation, since stemflow is deactivated, effective rainfall is equal to throughfall. In practical terms, PeffP_{\mathrm{eff}}Peff​ represents the amount of liquid water transferred from the atmosphere–canopy system to the land surface during the time step, prior to hydrodynamic routing.

If the interception routine is disabled, then no canopy storage is resolved and precipitation reaches the surface directly, so that

Peff=PgrossP_{\mathrm{eff}} = P_{\mathrm{gross}}Peff​=Pgross​

2.6 Subgrid rainfall correction for interception​

When subgrid and overbank options are active, the precipitation entering the interception module is scaled by the effective wet area:

Pint=ΔPagg Δx2CaP_{\mathrm{int}} = \Delta P_{\mathrm{agg}} \, \frac{\Delta x^2}{C_a}Pint​=ΔPagg​Ca​Δx2​

where:

  • PintP_{\mathrm{int}}Pint​ is precipitation used by the interception module [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • ΔPagg\Delta P_{\mathrm{agg}}ΔPagg​ is aggregated rainfall depth [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • Δx\Delta xΔx is grid resolution [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • CaC_aCa​ is effective wetted cell area [L2][\mathrm{L}^2][L2]

Otherwise,

Pint=ΔPaggP_{\mathrm{int}} = \Delta P_{\mathrm{agg}}Pint​=ΔPagg​

This correction ensures that interception remains consistent with the hydrologically active portion of the cell area under subgrid inundation or partial wetting conditions.

3. Potential Evapotranspiration and Evaporation​

HydroPol2D computes spatially distributed reference evapotranspiration using a Penman–Monteith formulation. Meteorological station data are first interpolated over the grid using inverse distance weighting, and the interpolated fields are then used to compute both reference evapotranspiration ETP\mathrm{ETP}ETP and an evaporation-like term EpE_{\mathrm{p}}Ep​.

The quantity ETP\mathrm{ETP}ETP is used as the standard reference evapotranspiration, while EpE_{\mathrm{p}}Ep​ is used in the interception routine to estimate evaporation from intercepted canopy water.

At each time step, the following fields are interpolated from available stations:

For a generic variable ϕ\phiϕ, the interpolated field at location x\mathbf{x}x is written conceptually as

ϕ(x)=∑i=1Nwi(x) ϕi∑i=1Nwi(x)\phi(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{ \sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i(\mathbf{x})\,\phi_i }{ \sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i(\mathbf{x}) }ϕ(x)=∑i=1N​wi​(x)∑i=1N​wi​(x)ϕi​​

3.4 Direct evaporation and transpiration forcing​

HydroPol2D can also use externally supplied raster fields of evaporation and transpiration. In that configuration, the model bypasses the internal Penman–Monteith calculation and applies prescribed EsurfE_{\mathrm{surf}}Esurf​ and TrT_{\mathrm{r}}Tr​ fields directly in the hydrologic mass balance.

4. Infiltration​

The infiltration module computes the transfer of water from the surface to the vadose zone using a Darcy-based formulation coupled with van Genuchten–Mualem constitutive relationships. The formulation is designed to approximate Richards-type unsaturated flow behavior while maintaining compatibility with the storage-based structure of HydroPol2D.

At each time step, infiltration is controlled by three simultaneous constraints:

  1. the availability of water at the surface,
  2. the hydraulic capacity of the soil,
  3. the remaining storage capacity of the vadose zone.

The final infiltration rate is determined as the minimum among these competing limits.

4.1 Water-table-controlled unsaturated storage​

The vertical structure of the soil column is dynamically linked to the groundwater table. The saturated thickness above the base of the soil profile is computed as

zgw=hgw−(zsurf−Dsoil)z_{\mathrm{gw}} = h_{\mathrm{gw}} - \left( z_{\mathrm{surf}} - D_{\mathrm{soil}} \right)zgw​=hgw​−(zsurf​−Dsoil​)

and the depth to the water table below the land surface is

zwt=Dsoil−zgwz_{\mathrm{wt}} = D_{\mathrm{soil}} - z_{\mathrm{gw}}zwt​=Dsoil​−zgw​

where:

  • hgwh_{\mathrm{gw}}hgw​ is groundwater head [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • zsurfz_{\mathrm{surf}}zsurf​ is land surface elevation [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • DsoilD_{\mathrm{soil}}Dsoil​ is soil depth [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • zwtz_{\mathrm{wt}}zwt​ is water-table depth below the surface [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

The maximum storage available in the vadose zone is then defined as

Suz,max=zwt(θsat−θi)S_{\mathrm{uz,max}} = z_{\mathrm{wt}} \left( \theta_{\mathrm{sat}} - \theta_{\mathrm{i}} \right)Suz,max​=zwt​(θsat​−θi​)

and the remaining storage capacity is

Suz,rem=max⁡(Suz,max−Suz, 0)S_{\mathrm{uz,rem}} = \max\left( S_{\mathrm{uz,max}} - S_{\mathrm{uz}},\,0 \right)Suz,rem​=max(Suz,max​−Suz​,0)

where:

  • Suz,maxS_{\mathrm{uz,max}}Suz,max​ is maximum vadose storage [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • SuzS_{\mathrm{uz}}Suz​ is current vadose storage [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • θi\theta_{\mathrm{i}}θi​ is initial/reference water content [−][-][−]
  • θsat\theta_{\mathrm{sat}}θsat​ is saturated water content [−][-][−]

This formulation ensures that the available storage dynamically responds to groundwater fluctuations. As the water table rises, zwtz_{\mathrm{wt}}zwt​ decreases and the vadose storage capacity shrinks accordingly.

  • Δhmax\Delta h_{\mathrm{max}}Δhmax​ is maximum driving head difference (typically 1 m1\,\mathrm{m}1m)

This formulation accounts for both gravity-driven and capillary-driven infiltration.

4.6 Supply-limited and storage-limited infiltration​

The water available for infiltration is determined from the effective surface water depth. When interception is inactive:

deff=dd_{\mathrm{eff}} = ddeff​=d

The corresponding supply-limited infiltration rate is

isup=deffΔthi_{\mathrm{sup}} = \frac{d_{\mathrm{eff}}}{\Delta t_{\mathrm{h}}}isup​=Δth​deff​​

where:

  • deffd_{\mathrm{eff}}deff​ is effective ponded water depth [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • Δth\Delta t_{\mathrm{h}}Δth​ is time step in hours [T][\mathrm{T}][T]

The storage-limited infiltration rate is

istore=Suz,remΔthi_{\mathrm{store}} = \frac{S_{\mathrm{uz,rem}}}{\Delta t_{\mathrm{h}}}istore​=Δth​Suz,rem​​

The actual infiltration rate is then

i=min⁡ ⁣(isup, Icap, istore)i = \min\!\left(i_{\mathrm{sup}},\,I_{\mathrm{cap}},\,i_{\mathrm{store}}\right)i=min(isup​,Icap​,istore​)

Additional constraints are applied:

  • i=0i = 0i=0 over impervious cells
  • i=0i = 0i=0 when zwt≤0z_{\mathrm{wt}} \le 0zwt​≤0

Thus, infiltration is simultaneously supply-limited, capacity-limited, and storage-limited.

4.7 Vadose storage update​

The infiltrated depth over the time step is

ΔI=i Δth\Delta I = i\,\Delta t_{\mathrm{h}}ΔI=iΔth​

and the vadose storage is updated as

Suz new=Suz+ΔIS_{\mathrm{uz}}^{\,\mathrm{new}} = S_{\mathrm{uz}} + \Delta ISuznew​=Suz​+ΔI

subject to

0≤Suz new≤Suz,max0 \le S_{\mathrm{uz}}^{\,\mathrm{new}} \le S_{\mathrm{uz,max}}0≤Suznew​≤Suz,max​

where:

  • Suz,max newS_{\mathrm{uz,max}}^{\,\mathrm{new}}Suz,maxnew​ is the updated maximum vadose-zone storage capacity [L][\mathrm{L}][L], recomputed at the current time step based on the updated water-table position.

5. Snow Accumulation, Melt, and Sublimation​

When snow modeling is active, precipitation is partitioned into rainfall and snowfall, and snowpack evolution is explicitly tracked through snow water equivalent (SWE), density, and depth.

5.1 Rain–snow partitioning​

Precipitation is partitioned based on air temperature:

Psnow=PgrossfsnowP_{\mathrm{snow}} = P_{\mathrm{gross}} f_{\mathrm{snow}}Psnow​=Pgross​fsnow​ Prain=Pgross−PsnowP_{\mathrm{rain}} = P_{\mathrm{gross}} - P_{\mathrm{snow}}Prain​=Pgross​−Psnow​

where fsnowf_{\mathrm{snow}}fsnow​ is a temperature-dependent snow fraction [−][-][−].

5.2 Snow water equivalent​

The evolution of snow water equivalent is

SWEt=SWEt−1+Psnow−Msnow−Es\mathrm{SWE}_t = \mathrm{SWE}_{t-1} + P_{\mathrm{snow}} - M_{\mathrm{snow}} - E_{\mathrm{s}}SWEt​=SWEt−1​+Psnow​−Msnow​−Es​

where:

  • SWE\mathrm{SWE}SWE is snow water equivalent [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • EsE_{\mathrm{s}}Es​ is sublimation [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

5.3 Snowmelt​

Snowmelt is computed as

Msnow=max⁡(DDF Tair+(1−αsnow) Qnet334,0)M_{\mathrm{snow}} = \max \left( \mathrm{DDF}\,T_{\mathrm{air}} + \frac{(1-\alpha_{\mathrm{snow}})\,Q_{\mathrm{net}}}{334}, 0 \right)Msnow​=max(DDFTair​+334(1−αsnow​)Qnet​​,0)

subject to

Msnow≤SWEtM_{\mathrm{snow}} \le \mathrm{SWE}_tMsnow​≤SWEt​

where:

  • DDF\mathrm{DDF}DDF is degree-day factor [L Θ−1 T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\Theta^{-1}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LΘ−1T−1]
  • αsnow\alpha_{\mathrm{snow}}αsnow​ is snow albedo [−][-][−] that can be either assumed as constant or entered as spatial input in the General_Data.xlsx.
  • QnetQ_{\mathrm{net}}Qnet​ is net energy flux available for snowmelt [E L−2 T−1][\mathrm{E}\,\mathrm{L}^{-2}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][EL−2T−1], representing the combined radiative and turbulent energy exchanges at the snow surface.

The current snow parameters must be edited in the function HydroPol2D_Preprocessing.m.

5.4 Snow density and depth​

Snow density evolves as

ρsnow new=ρsnow old+ktTair+ksweSWE+kDHsnow\rho_{\mathrm{snow}}^{\,\mathrm{new}} = \rho_{\mathrm{snow}}^{\,\mathrm{old}} + k_t T_{\mathrm{air}} + k_{\mathrm{swe}} \mathrm{SWE} + k_D H_{\mathrm{snow}}ρsnownew​=ρsnowold​+kt​Tair​+kswe​SWE+kD​Hsnow​

subject to

ρsnow≤ρmax\rho_{\mathrm{snow}} \le \rho_{\mathrm{max}}ρsnow​≤ρmax​

Snow depth is

Hsnow=SWEρsnowH_{\mathrm{snow}} = \frac{\mathrm{SWE}}{\rho_{\mathrm{snow}}}Hsnow​=ρsnow​SWE​

5.5 Sublimation​

Es=Ce u (qs−qa) SWEE_{\mathrm{s}} = C_e\,u\,(q_s - q_a)\,\mathrm{SWE}Es​=Ce​u(qs​−qa​)SWE

where:

  • CeC_eCe​ is sublimation coefficient [−][-][−]
  • uuu is wind speed [L T−1][\mathrm{L}\,\mathrm{T}^{-1}][LT−1], taken as the wind speed at 2 m2\,\mathrm{m}2m height.
  • qsq_sqs​ is saturation specific humidity at the snow surface [−][-][−]
  • qaq_aqa​ is ambient air specific humidity [−][-][−]

5.6 Surface-water update under snow conditions​

dt=dp+Msnow+Praind_t = d_p + M_{\mathrm{snow}} + P_{\mathrm{rain}}dt​=dp​+Msnow​+Prain​

otherwise

dt=dp+Peffd_t = d_p + P_{\mathrm{eff}}dt​=dp​+Peff​

where:

  • dtd_tdt​ is surface water depth at the current time step [L][\mathrm{L}][L]
  • dpd_pdp​ is surface water depth at the previous time step [L][\mathrm{L}][L]

6. Vadose Storage, Recharge, and Groundwater Feedback​

6.1 Recharge​

Recharge is computed dynamically using Darcy's law assuming a representative flow width to reach the aquifer free surface

Rgw=K(θ)(1+ψmLgw)R_{\mathrm{gw}} = K(\theta) \left( 1 + \frac{\psi_{\mathrm{m}}}{L_{\mathrm{gw}}} \right)Rgw​=K(θ)(1+Lgw​ψm​​)

with

Lgw=0.5 zwtL_{\mathrm{gw}} = 0.5\,z_{\mathrm{wt}}Lgw​=0.5zwt​

Therefore, the current version considers half of the depth to the aquifer distance as the representative distance to compute the recharge.

6.2 Storage evolution​

By simply computing a reservoir mass balance with a forward explicit Euler numerical scheme, one can obtain:

Suz,t+1=Suz,t+Δt(i−Rgw)S_{\mathrm{uz},t+1} = S_{\mathrm{uz},t} + \Delta t \left( i - R_{\mathrm{gw}} \right)Suz,t+1​=Suz,t​+Δt(i−Rgw​)

where Δt\Delta tΔt is in seconds.

6.3 Mass-consistent recharge​

Rgw=i−Suz,t+1−Suz,tΔtR_{\mathrm{gw}} = i - \frac{ S_{\mathrm{uz},t+1} - S_{\mathrm{uz},t} }{\Delta t}Rgw​=i−ΔtSuz,t+1​−Suz,t​​

6.4 Saturation excess​

Sexcess=max⁡(Suz−Suz,max new,0)S_{\mathrm{excess}} = \max \left( S_{\mathrm{uz}} - S_{\mathrm{uz,max}}^{\,\mathrm{new}}, 0 \right)Sexcess​=max(Suz​−Suz,maxnew​,0)

6.5 Groundwater exfiltration​

qexf=max⁡(0, hgw−zsurf)SyΔtq_{\mathrm{exf}} = \max \left( 0,\, h_{\mathrm{gw}} - z_{\mathrm{surf}} \right) \frac{S_y}{\Delta t}qexf​=max(0,hgw​−zsurf​)ΔtSy​​

where:

  • SyS_ySy​ is specific yield [−][-][−]

7. Summary​

The hydrologic model in HydroPol2D integrates:

  • canopy interception,
  • Penman–Monteith evapotranspiration,
  • Darcy-based infiltration,
  • snow accumulation and melt,
  • vadose storage and groundwater coupling,

into a unified, mass-consistent framework that resolves vertical water fluxes at the grid-cell scale and provides the hydrologic forcing for the hydrodynamic model.
